Taking part in the **2024 Intel Student Ambassador Fall Hackathon** was a chance to push on both classical ML and quantum-inspired modelling. With teammates Sreeneha Samudrala Snighdha (data science, Vienna) and Nwankwo Chijioke (front-end, Chicago), we built a **Quantum Neural Network (QNN)** pipeline aimed at **brain image analysis**.

## The problem

Precise segmentation and evaluation of brain images matter for diagnosing neurological conditions. We wanted to explore whether quantum-enhanced layers could complement classical networks—especially when paired with **Intel’s optimisation and deployment stack**.

## What we used

- **Intel Tiber™ AI Cloud** for VMs and repeatable experiments  
- **TorchQuantum**, **ONNX**, **OpenVINO™** for conversion and efficient inference  
- A hybrid architecture with a **Quanvolution**-style filter to process selected patches

## Outcomes

The work was as much about **integration and measurement** as about novelty: converting models to ONNX/OpenVINO, tuning runs on CPU, and documenting setups so Vienna and Chicago stayed in sync.
